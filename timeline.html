A Timeline of Indian History

From Pre-Historic Ages to the Mughal Empire

Pre-History & Metal Ages

Palaeolithic & Mesolithic Ages

c. 500,000 – 6000 BCE

The Old and Late Stone Ages, characterized by hunting, gathering, and the first signs of herding and rock art.

Neolithic & Chalcolithic Ages

c. 6000 – 500 BCE

The New Stone Age brought food production, while the Copper-Stone Age introduced the use of metal alongside stone tools.

Ancient Civilizations & Empires

Harappan Civilisation

c. 2600 – 1900 BCE

South Asia's first urban, Bronze Age civilization, with distinct early, mature, and late phases. Evidence suggests origins may be as old as 8,000 years.

The Vedic Age

c. 1500 – 500 BCE

Era defined by Vedic texts. The Early period (Rig Vedic) was in the Indus region, while the Later period saw Aryan expansion into the Gangetic plains.

The Mahajanapadas

c. 600 – 300 BCE

The "Second Urbanisation." Magadha rose to power under the Haryanka, Shishunaga, and Nanda dynasties. This era also saw Persian and Macedonian invasions.

The Maurya Empire

c. 324 – 187 BCE

Founded by Chandragupta Maurya, it was the first sub-continental empire. Ashoka the Great is its most famous ruler, known for his policy of Dhamma.

Post-Mauryan & Classical Era

Post-Mauryan Kingdoms

c. 200 BCE – 300 CE

A complex period with many regional powers, including the Sungas, Kanvas, Indo-Greeks, Shakas, Kushanas, and the Satavahanas in the Deccan.

Sangam Age (South India)

c. 3rd century BCE – 3rd century CE

The era of the Chera, Chola, and Pandya kingdoms in the southern part of the subcontinent, known for trade with the Romans.

The Gupta Empire

c. 300 – 600 CE

Considered the "Golden Age." Founded by Sri Gupta, with great rulers like Chandragupta I, Samudragupta, and Chandragupta II (Vikramaditya). Contemporaneous with the Vakatakas in the Deccan.

Early Medieval India

Age of Regional Configurations

c. 600 – 750 CE

After the Guptas, regional powers like the Pushyabhutis (notably Harsha Vardhana), Pallavas of Kanchi, and Chalukyas of Badami rose to prominence.

The Tripartite Struggle

c. 750 – 1000 CE

A long conflict for control of northern India between the Pratiharas, the Palas of Bengal, and the Rashtrakutas of the Deccan.

Rise of Rajput Kingdoms

c. 1000 – 1200 CE

The breakdown of larger powers led to the emergence of smaller Rajput kingdoms, including the Chauhans, Chandellas, Paramaras, and Tomaras.

Sultanates & The Mughal Empire

The Delhi Sultanate

c. 1206 – 1526 CE

A series of five dynasties (Slave, Khalji, Tughlaq, Saiyyad, Lodhi) that ruled from Delhi, marking a new era in the subcontinent's history.

Provincial Medieval Kingdoms

c. 1336 – 1672 CE

With the decline of the Sultanate, powerful regional kingdoms emerged, most notably the Vijayanagar Empire and the Bahmani Sultanate in the South.

The Mughal Empire

c. 1526 – 1857 CE

Founded by Babur, it became one of the world's largest empires. Akbar's reign marked a period of consolidation. The empire declined after Aurangzeb, leading to the rise of the Marathas and other provincial powers.
